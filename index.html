Before chocolate becomes sweet,
it begins bitter.

Just like most honest things.

Taken from the earth,
handled with care.

Because rushing ruins flavor.

Heat changes everything.
But only when it’s controlled.

Too much burns.
Too little leaves it raw.

Ground slowly.
Turned again and again.

Until bitterness learns softness.

Only after patience,
does sweetness take shape.



Take your time.

This isn’t a confession.
It isn’t a request.

It’s just a moment,
wrapped gently.

— with intention
